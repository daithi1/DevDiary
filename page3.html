Entry #3

Third log: 18/02/26

So from the plan we had made (shown in the previous log), I wanted to first create an AI for our zombies so that they would track the player wherever they were on the map. Now I had no idea the treat I was in for trying to make this. Looking back on it, coming from zero experience with coding a game I think creating a zombie AI might have been a bit too ambitious, for me at least. Before I was even able to open the Unity editor, I already encountered an issue. I have recently switched to linux for the first time and am still quite clueless to how it works, so getting a working unity editor was the first hurdle! Eventually I did get it to work though, and I begun working on the AI. As a start, I followed various youtube tutorials on making a zombie AI in 3D games in Unity.
These are the 2 youtube videos I tried out:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UjkSFoLxesw&t
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yai-RscB8FA&t
The "issue" I encountered with both of these was getting a player that the zombies would follow. The only reason I'm calling this an issue is because I got way too caught up with making a working player script so that I could move and around and test. I unfortuntately didn't get the zombie AI done today but I did get working player movement! This will hopefully make future testing more useful...
Other progress:
In other news during our meeting today, one of our coders implemented basic player movement and dodging! The dodge is just a simple roll but quite impressive for a first iteration in my opinion.
Ont top of that, one of our artists had done some basic sketches of various GUI elements that will be seen throughout the game. Although they were only simple sketches it helped give us an idea of how our game would look.
