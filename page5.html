Fifth log: 06/03/26

Progress hasn't been looking the best lately to be completely honest. So today we decided to spend a good amount of time on call together tidying up some things and finally putting together our first "official" build of the game. Since last meeting (2 days ago), I had put together somewhat of a working zombie AI that I was actually a bit proud of. It wasn't the best by any means but it got the job done for the most part. With my AI and everyone else's work put together we managed to make our first proper iteration of our game. Although quite simple, we were really proud of ourselves for the effort we put in and the fact that we actually had a working product that we could look at and admire. That day we had spent an arguably obscene amount of time on call but it was DEFINITELY worth it.
My zombie AI:

Below is the beginning of the best zombie AI in the whole wide world. Well not yet but I'm reaching high.
The player (capsule) is controlled by you of course, which is then simply chased by the zombie. I of course haven't added animation yet but that is a work in progress. The red flashing on the player is to show that the player is invincible, giving you i-frames. That's how you know this game is gonna be good. To make this I found out about a lovely thing in Unity called NavMesh. This basically sets up a map for you of where the enemy can travel and pathfind to you with ease. It did take me a little bit too long to figure this out but eventually I did and I am definitely glad I found it.
